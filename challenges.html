Challenges

Lack of medical facilities: In Northeastern Kenya, many areas are devoid of adequate medical facilities. For example, the town of Mandera has only one major hospital serving a large population. This makes it difficult for residents to receive timely and effective medical care.

Poor infrastructure: The road networks in this region are often in poor condition, making travel difficult. During the rainy season, many roads become impassable, which isolates communities from essential services. An example is the road from Garissa to Wajir, which is notorious for its poor state.

Insufficient healthcare professionals: The region suffers from a severe shortage of healthcare workers. For instance, many health centers in remote areas have no doctors, relying instead on nurses and community health workers, who are often overburdened.

Limited access to medication: Pharmacies and medical supplies are scarce. A typical scenario is found in the town of Garbatulla, where essential medications are often out of stock, forcing patients to travel long distances to obtain their prescriptions.

Financial constraints: Many residents live below the poverty line and cannot afford healthcare. The high cost of medical services and transportation exacerbates their inability to seek timely medical attention, as seen in the widespread poverty in the region.
